## What's happening

April has been intense. On the 7th I upgraded to Claude Code Max and the same day started two new projects: Padel Up and MarketMyApp. Creator Roast came shortly after. Now I'm running four active things in parallel — which would have felt impossible three months ago.

## What's shipping

**Padel Up** — the all-in-one padel iOS app is done. Landing page is live at [trypadelup.com](https://www.trypadelup.com/). Waiting on App Store approval. The product is ready, just in the queue.

**MarketMyApp** — building. The pitch: stop drowning in marketing frameworks and let AI tell you the three highest-leverage things to do this week. Sole focus after Padel Up launches.

**Creator Roast** — building in parallel. AI that roasts your creator profile and tells you exactly what's broken. Simple concept, high demand.

**Helpmarq** — running quietly at [helpmarq.com](https://helpmarq.com). Not pushing growth right now, letting it find its own pace while I focus on the faster-moving products.

## Biggest change this month

Claude Code Max. The difference from the standard version is significant enough to change how I structure work. I'm delegating more, planning before I touch code, and trusting the agents on medium-complexity tasks instead of doing everything inline. It compounds fast.

## Reading

*The Mom Test* by Rob Fitzpatrick. If you build things and talk to potential users — read this. It's short, specific, and will change how you run every customer conversation.

## Tool I can't live without

Warp terminal. Once you use it, going back to a standard terminal feels painful. The AI command lookup alone saves me 20 minutes a week of Googling flags.
